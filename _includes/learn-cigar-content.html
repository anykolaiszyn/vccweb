## Cigar Basics 101 ### Anatomy - Wrapper: outer leaf that drives first flavor impressions - Binder: structural leaf holding the cigar together - Filler: blend core that defines complexity and strength ### Popular Sizes - Robusto: short and balanced - Toro: longer and versatile - Churchill: long-format, slower pace - Corona: classic and refined ### Flavor Guide - Mild: creamy, nutty, light spice - Medium: balanced earth, cocoa, cedar - Full: richer pepper, dark chocolate, and depth ### Pairing Basics - Mild cigars pair well with coffee or lighter spirits - Medium cigars pair with whiskey or red wine - Full cigars pair with aged rum or bourbon ### Beginner Tips 1. Start with mild-to-medium selections 2. Take slow draws and do not rush 3. Ask for cut and lighting guidance 4. Track flavors you enjoy to refine future picks ### Intro to Pipes - Pipe smoking is slower and ritual-driven - Start with a simple pipe and mild blend - Pack gently, light evenly, and sip slowly
